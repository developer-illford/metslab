What European and international standards do we follow?

Following the UK's transition period, our testing protocols maintain full alignment with requirements established by the Vehicle Certification Agency (VCA) and the Department for Transport (DfT). We operate under the comprehensive framework of the Road Vehicles (Approval) Regulations 2020, which established the GB Type Approval Scheme. This framework encompasses crucial testing requirements for emissions, safety standards, and electromagnetic compatibility, ensuring vehicles meet both domestic and international requirements for road worthiness.

The Road Vehicle Construction and Use Regulations form the cornerstone of our testing methodology, addressing fundamental safety and environmental standards for vehicles used on UK roads. These regulations guide our testing processes for essential aspects including lighting systems, signaling mechanisms, exhaust emissions, and noise levels, while the Motor Vehicle Type Approval Regulation provides the framework for comprehensive testing of both new vehicle designs and modifications to existing models. Through our continued participation in the UN ECE 1958 and 1998 Agreements, we maintain harmonization with global standards while satisfying specific UK requirements, particularly in areas such as steering equipment, braking systems, and environmental performance testing.

Our accreditation from UKAS (United Kingdom Accreditation Service) demonstrates our commitment to maintaining testing excellence under ISO/IEC 17025 requirements for testing laboratories. For electric and hybrid vehicles, we conduct specialized testing following the Electric Vehicles (Smart Charge Points) Regulations 2021, ensuring charging systems meet current UK safety and compatibility requirements. Additionally, our testing protocols align with the UN Regulation on Cybersecurity and Software Updates (UN R155 and R156), which the UK has adopted, ensuring vehicles meet modern cybersecurity challenges while maintaining functional safety in an increasingly connected automotive landscape.

What are the key testing areas and technical focus?
