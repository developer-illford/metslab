Water & Wastewater Testing Categories :
1. Drinking Water

Clean drinking water is essential for life, and ensuring its safety and quality is one of our top priorities.

  • Potable Water: Potable water must meet strict health and safety criteria to ensure it is safe for human consumption. At our lab, we conduct comprehensive tests to detect microbial pathogens, heavy metals, chemical residues, and physical impurities. Our analysis also includes taste, odor, and turbidity assessments, ensuring your water is not only safe but also enjoyable to consume.
  • Bottled Water: The bottled water industry demands precision and compliance to maintain consumer trust. We test bottled water varieties such as still, sparkling, mineral, and flavored for purity, chemical composition, and microbiological safety. Our process ensures compliance with local and international quality standards, giving you confidence in every bottle.
  • Reverse Osmosis (RO) Water: RO water systems are increasingly popular for their ability to remove impurities. However, periodic testing is critical to ensure optimal performance. We analyze RO water for residual contaminants, ensuring the filtration process is functioning efficiently and meeting safety requirements.
2. Recreational Water

Water used for recreational activities requires regular monitoring to ensure it is safe and hygienic for public use.

  • Swimming Pool Water: Pools must maintain the right balance of disinfectants, pH levels, and turbidity to prevent skin irritation and microbial contamination. We conduct thorough tests to assess chlorine levels, microbial content, and overall water quality, guaranteeing compliance with health standards.
  • Spa and Hot Tub Water: Hot tubs and spas are ideal environments for bacteria to thrive due to high temperatures. We monitor critical factors like water temperature, disinfectant levels, and microbial presence to ensure a relaxing and safe user experience.
  • Natural Recreational Water: Lakes, rivers, and seas used for recreational purposes are vulnerable to pollutants and pathogens. Our testing identifies contaminants, such as chemical toxins, harmful algal blooms, and microbial hazards, safeguarding public health and preserving natural ecosystems.
3. Industrial Water

Water quality in industrial settings directly impacts operational efficiency, product quality, and equipment lifespan.

  • Boiler Feed Water: Boilers require water free from impurities that cause scaling, corrosion, or inefficiencies. We test for hardness, silica content, dissolved oxygen, and other critical parameters to ensure the water meets operational requirements.
  • Cooling Tower Water: Cooling systems rely on clean water to prevent scaling, microbial growth, and corrosion. We evaluate factors such as water alkalinity, bacterial presence, and chemical inhibitors to maintain system performance and reduce maintenance costs.
  • Process Water: Process water is a vital component in manufacturing and production lines. Our testing ensures that the water used in specific industrial processes meets purity and safety requirements, minimizing contamination risks and ensuring product consistency.
4. Agricultural and Irrigation Water

Water quality in agriculture affects crop yields, soil health, and livestock well-being.

  • Irrigation Water: Irrigation water must be free of contaminants that could harm crops or alter soil chemistry. We test for salinity, nutrient content, and chemical residues, providing insights to maximize agricultural productivity while protecting the environment.
  • Livestock Water: Access to clean water is essential for animal health and growth. Our testing services identify microbial contaminants, toxins, and other impurities, ensuring livestock stay healthy and productive.
5. Environmental Water

Monitoring natural water sources is essential for environmental protection and sustainable resource management.

  • Surface Water: Surface water, such as rivers, lakes, and reservoirs, is susceptible to pollution and eutrophication. We conduct detailed assessments of these water bodies, evaluating chemical pollutants, nutrient imbalances, and ecological health risks.
  • Groundwater: Groundwater, drawn from wells and aquifers, is a vital resource for many communities. We test for common contaminants such as nitrates, arsenic, fluoride, and heavy metals, providing actionable recommendations to safeguard public health.
  • Stormwater: Stormwater runoff can carry a variety of pollutants, including oil, heavy metals, and sediments. Our testing helps municipalities and industries manage runoff effectively, ensuring compliance with environmental guidelines and reducing risks to ecosystems.
6. Wastewater

Stormwater runoff can carry a variety of pollutants, including oil, heavy metals, and sediments. Our testing helps municipalities and industries manage runoff effectively, ensuring compliance with environmental guidelines and reducing risks to ecosystems.

  • Municipal Wastewater: Municipal wastewater contains organic matter, nutrients, and pathogens. We test for key indicators such as BOD, COD, TSS, and microbial contaminants, ensuring treatment facilities meet regulatory discharge requirements.
  • Industrial Wastewater: Industrial effluents vary depending on the sector but often contain hazardous substances. We analyze wastewater for heavy metals, oils, chemicals, and specific pollutants, offering customized solutions for regulatory compliance.
  • Greywater: Greywater recycling is an innovative way to conserve water. We test for organic content, microbial safety, and chemical residues, ensuring recycled water is suitable for irrigation and other non-potable uses.
7. Specialized Water Types

Certain industries require water with extremely high purity standards.

  • Deionized Water: Used in laboratories and manufacturing, deionized water must have minimal ionic content. Our testing ensures conductivity and purity levels meet stringent requirements.
  • Distilled Water: Distilled water, essential for medical and industrial processes, is analyzed for contaminants, pH levels, and dissolved solids to guarantee its suitability for critical applications.
  • Ultra-Pure Water: Ultra-pure water is vital in sectors like pharmaceuticals and semiconductors. We test for ionic, organic, and microbial contaminants, ensuring compliance with the strictest industry standards.
8. Recreational or Tourism-Specific Water

Tourism-based water attractions must prioritize safety and aesthetics.

  • Theme Park Water: Artificial water bodies like lagoons, fountains, and rides require regular testing for chemical balance, microbial safety, and clarity to enhance visitor satisfaction.
  • Marine Park Water: Water used to sustain aquatic life in marine parks must meet specific salinity, nutrient, and microbial requirements. We help maintain optimal conditions for marine ecosystems.
9. Public Health and Emergency Water

In emergencies, access to safe water is critical for survival.

  • Emergency Water Supplies: During crises, water safety is paramount. Mets Lab provides rapid testing for microbial and chemical safety, ensuring emergency water supplies are fit for consumption.
  • Hospital and Healthcare Water: Healthcare facilities require high-quality water for patient care. We test for microbial safety, chemical residues, and compliance with healthcare standards, ensuring safety across all applications.
