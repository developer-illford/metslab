Standards We Follow

The laboratory maintains ISO/IEC 17025:2017 accreditation which verifies its expert capabilities for testing services. The laboratory follows testing methods from ASTM International together with protocols from International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and Energy Institute (EI) standard procedures. Our testing procedures fulfill worldwide requirements through internationally recognized standards which produce accurate consistent results.

All testing protocols at our facility receive UKAS validation in accordance with UK regulations through the Energy Act 2016 standards. Our organization maintains close interaction with the Health and Safety Executive (HSE) to follow the Environmental Protection Act 1990 guidelines. We maintain participation in the UK Offshore Chemical Notification Scheme to provide a full range of testing services which meets all regulatory demands of our client base.

When performing specific tests we use ASTM D1298 for density measurements as well as ASTM D445 for kinematic viscosity and ASTM D4294 for sulfur content measurement. The laboratory executes petroleum sample collection through BS EN ISO 3170 manual testing procedures while using EN ISO 4259 analysis methods to achieve reliable test results.

Environmental protection drives our testing approach. We maintain compliance with Environment Agency protocols and SEPA (Scottish Environment Protection Agency) requirements. Our testing frameworks align with Department for Business, Energy & Industrial Strategy (BEIS) guidelines and Oil & Gas Authority (OGA) specifications. Additionally, we follow International Maritime Organization (IMO) regulations for marine fuels, ensuring comprehensive coverage of environmental and safety standards.

This robust framework of standards and accreditations ensures that every test, analysis, and report meets industry requirements while supporting our clients’ operational and environmental goals. Why is Testing Oil and Gas Products Important?

The crude oil & hydrocarbon analysis is a critical field within the petroleum industry that involves the assessment of hydrocarbon compositions, properties, and behaviors. As global energy demands continue to rise and the complexities of crude oil production deepen, the need for accurate and comprehensive analysis becomes essential to ensure.

  • Product Quality & Performance: Maintaining the integrity of crude oil, fuels, and petrochemicals.
  • Hydrocarbon Content: Hydrocarbon testing and analysis assist oil and gas organizations in identifying and analyzing the concentration of hydrocarbon compounds. Refiners can determine the best processing methods to maximize yield and minimize the cost.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Meeting global standards such as ASTM, ISO, and regional specifications.
  • Environmental Protection: Monitoring emissions, wastewater, and soil contamination.
  • Operational Safety: Detecting hazardous components, corrosion risks, and material degradation.
  • Process Optimization: Enhancing refining, storage, and transportation efficiency.

Categories of Oil & Gas Testing:
Crude Oil and Its Derivatives
  • Crude Oil Samples: An evaluation between sweet and sour oil and between light and heavy crude for their suitability in refining operations.
  • Refined Petroleum Products: Quality verification for gasoline, diesel, jet fuel, and kerosene.
  • Lubricants & Oils: Laboratory staff determines viscosity and performance levels of engine oils in addition to hydraulic oils and gear oils.
  • Asphalt/Bitumen: Evaluating properties for use in construction and industrial applications.
Petrochemicals
  • Basic Petrochemicals: Ethylene, propylene, butadiene, benzene, toluene, and xylene (BTX).
  • Intermediate Chemicals: Methanol, acetic acid, and ethylene glycol for industrial applications.
  • Polymers & Resins: Testing polyethylene, polypropylene, and PVC properties.
Industrial Gases
  • Hydrogen Gas: Purity assessment for industrial and energy applications.
  • Oxygen, Nitrogen & Argon: Ensuring medical and industrial-grade gas purity.
Exploration and Drilling Fluids
  • Drilling Fluids (Mud): Testing viscosity, density, and chemical stability of oil-based, water-based, and synthetic-based mud.
  • Completion Fluids: Assessing brines and specialized fluids for well completion.
  • Produced Water: Analyzing oil content, salinity, and heavy metal contamination.
Refinery and Production By-Products
  • Sulfur Recovery: Measuring elemental sulfur obtained during refining.
  • Petroleum Coke: Evaluating coke quality for industrial applications.
  • Waste Products: Testing sludge, effluent water, and spent catalysts.
Environmental Monitoring
  • Emissions Testing: Measuring CO, CO₂, NOₓ, SOₓ, and VOC levels in flue gas.
  • Wastewater Analysis: Monitoring effluent for oil and grease, total dissolved solids (TDS), and chemical oxygen demand (COD/BOD).
  • Soil Contamination: Detecting hydrocarbon and heavy metal presence in spill sites.
Corrosion and Material Testing
  • Corrosion Inhibitors: Evaluating the efficiency and compatibility of corrosion prevention agents.
  • Pipeline & Equipment Testing: Assessing metal and coating durability in oil and gas environments.
Fuels and Combustion Testing
  • Fuel Analysis: Determining octane rating (gasoline), cetane number (diesel), flash point, viscosity, and sulfur content.
  • Biofuels: Evaluating biodiesel, ethanol blends, and alternative fuel quality.
Specialty Oil & Gas Products
  • Additives: Testing antioxidants, corrosion inhibitors, and lubricity agents.
  • Catalysts: Evaluating refining catalysts for hydrocracking and reforming processes.
Hazardous & Safety-Related Testing
  • Explosives & Flammability: Measuring vapor pressure and ignition risks.
  • Toxic Gas Detection: Assessing hydrogen sulfide (H₂S) levels and toxicity risks in oil and gas streams.
Storage and Transport Analysis
  • Tank & Pipeline Sampling: Detecting residual oil, sludge, and contamination.
  • Marine Fuel Testing: Ensuring bunker fuel compatibility and stability for shipping.
Renewable & Alternative Fuels
  • Synthetic Fuels: Testing gas-to-liquid (GTL) and coal-to-liquid (CTL) derived fuels.
  • Hydrogen & Ammonia: Evaluating alternative energy carriers for future sustainability.
Regulatory Compliance Testing
  • International Standards: Compliance with ASTM, ISO, and IP testing methodologies.
  • Regional Regulations: Ensuring adherence to EU fuel standards, US EPA guidelines, and other national regulations.
