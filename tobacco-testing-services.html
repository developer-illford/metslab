Tobacco Products Directive 2014/40/EU

The Tobacco Products Directive (2014/40/EU) came into force on 19 May 2014 and was implemented in EU member states on 20 May 2016.The Directive establishes rules for the production, presentation and sale of tobacco and tobacco-related products.These are cigarettes, roll your own tobacco, pipe tobacco, cigars, cigarillos, smokeless tobacco, electronic cigarettes and herbal products for smoking

In particular, as per the Directive;
  • Prohibits cigarettes and roll-your-own tobacco with characterizing flavors
  • Compels tobacco companies to report to EU nations about ingredients of tobacco products
  • Compels tobacco and tobacco-related products to carry health warnings: combined health warnings (image, text, and information on how to quit) must be placed on at least 65% of the front and rear of cigarette and roll-your-own tobacco packs.
  • Defines minimum sizes for warnings and bans small packets for some tobacco products
  • Prohibits promotional and deceptive features on tobacco products, e-cigarettes, and herbal smoking products
  • Establishes EU-wide traceability and tracking to fight illicit trade in tobacco products
  • Enables EU member states to ban online sales of tobacco and tobacco-related products
  • Establishes safety, quality, and notification standards for electronic cigarettes
  • Requires manufacturers and importers to inform EU member states of new tobacco products prior to making them available on the EU market
Fire Safety Requirements for Cigarettes:
  • The European Union adopted decision 2008/264/EC on fire safety requirements to be met by European standards for cigarettes under the product safety Directive 2001/95/EC.
  • All cigarettes manufactured and sold in Europe were required to be compliant with the standard that no more than 25% of a batch of cigarette specimens tested shall burn through their whole length.
  • Reduced ignition risk: Cigarettes include ultra-thin concentric bands or 'speed bumps' that restrict oxygen access to the burning end of the cigarettes, causing the cigarette to die out if not 'puffed' by the user, lowering the risk of fire.

Amends UK legislation Tobacco Advertising and Promotion Act 2002, Tobacco Advertising and Promotion (Brandsharing) Regulations 2004, The Standardized Packaging of Tobacco Regulations 2015, The Nicotine Inhaling Products (Age of Sale and Proxy Purchasing) Regulations 2015, and Tobacco and Related Products Regulations 2016 and amends or repeals EU legislation 2015/1842, 2016/779, 2015/2183, 2015/2186, and 2016/786 all concerning tobacco packaging and labelling, product contents and flavors, and advertising and promotion in light of UK’s EU exit; only provisions on product contents and flavors included below. Made on April 18, 2016, UK-wide, but Scotland is debating whether to go further; went into effect on May 20, 2016, with a sell-through period ending on May 21, 2017. Categories of Tobacco Products:

We specialize in the testing and analysis of all major categories of tobacco products:

1. Smoking Tobacco
  • Cigarettes: Filtered, unfiltered, regular, menthol, and flavored types.
  • Cigars: Large cigars, cigarillos, and little cigars in hand-rolled and machine-made formats.
  • Pipe Tobacco: Custom blends for traditional pipe use.
  • Roll-Your-Own (RYO) Tobacco: Loose tobacco for personalized cigarette creation.
2. Smokeless Tobacco
  • Chewing Tobacco: Loose leaf, plug, and twist forms.
  • Snuff: Moist snuff (oral use) and dry snuff (nasal or oral use).
  • Dissolvable Tobacco: Lozenges, strips, and pellets that dissolve in the mouth.
  • Snus: Moist pouches discreetly placed under the upper lip.
3. Alternative Tobacco Products
  • Hookah Tobacco (Shisha): Fruit-based along with spice-based and herbal blends serve as smoking additives for water pipes.
  • Heat-Not-Burn (HNB) Tobacco: Tobacco sticks or capsules used in non-combustion devices.
4. Electronic Nicotine Delivery Systems (ENDS)
  • E-Cigarettes: Disposable and rechargeable devices.
  • Vape Devices: Open (refillable) and closed (pre-filled) systems.
  • E-Liquids: The product line includes nicotine-containing and nicotine-free vaping options with multiple flavor choices.
5. Specialty Tobacco Products
  • Bidis: Small, hand-rolled, often flavored cigarettes.
  • Kreteks: Clove-infused cigarettes combining tobacco with spices.
  • Herbal Cigarettes: Tobacco-free products made from herbal blends.
6. Industrial and Raw Tobacco
  • Tobacco Leaves: Green and partially on brass stripped leaf for industrial or personal use.
  • Tobacco Extracts and Concentrates: For use in flavoring and additive.
  • By-products such as tobacco dust which turn into agricultural rest yields.
7. Flavored and Enhanced Products
  • Flavored Tobacco: Infused with flavors like vanilla, cherry, or mint.
  • Tobacco Wraps: Flavored sheets for rolling cigars or cigarettes.
  • Mentholated Products: Tobacco enhanced with menthol for a cooling sensation.
8. Medicinal and Non-Recreational Products
  • Nicotine Replacement Products: Patches, gums, and lozenges derived from tobacco for cessation purposes.
9. Cultural and Traditional Tobacco
  • Traditional Use: Tobacco in its native form or only minimally altered state exists for ceremonial and cultural purposes.
  • Betel Quid with Tobacco: A blend of betel leaf, areca nut, and slaked lime with tobacco.
10. Accessories for Tobacco Products
  • Rolling Papers and Filters: Essentials for crafting cigarettes.
  • Devices: Pipes, hookahs, and ENDS components.
  • Vape Accessories: Coils, tanks, and chargers for electronic nicotine devices.
