What European and international standards do we follow?

Our Construction Materials Testing Lab align with the most rigorous European and international requirements. We maintain full compliance with BS EN Standards (British Standards European Norms), including BS EN 206 for concrete specification and performance, BS EN 13369 for precast concrete products, BS EN 12620 for concrete aggregates, and BS EN 10080 for reinforcement steel. Additionally, our operations are certified under key ISO standards, including ISO 9001:2015 for Quality Management, ISO 14001 for Environmental Management, and ISO 45001 for Occupational Health and Safety.

The testing procedures at our Construction Materials Testing Lab implement complete BS EN ISO/IEC 17025 standards that guide testing and calibration operations. According to BS EN 12390 the laboratory conducts testing on concrete after following specific guidelines that include both specimen preparation steps and defined tests for compressive strength evaluation and tensile strength measurement and density determination. Our aggregate testing methods follow exact standards outlined in BS EN 932 and BS EN 933 series for defining geometric and mechanical properties. Our material testing procedures demonstrate consistent quality and reliability throughout every testing process we perform.

The testing of steel and metal products according to BS EN ISO 6892-1 specifies the method of tensile testing metallic materials at room temperature. We perform detailed testing that includes both mechanical properties evaluation along with steel reinforcement dimensional assessments that comply with BS EN 10080. Our testing equipment must undergo regular calibration and maintenance according to UKAS (United Kingdom Accreditation Service) standards for achieving the highest accuracy levels. Through a complete testing method engineers can verify that all materials achieve and surpass the essential performance criteria for constructing in the UK.
