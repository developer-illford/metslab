What European and international standards do we follow?

The Testing Services adheres to premier regulatory frameworks of United Kingdom through its compliance with the Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA). The Testing Services maintains GLP monitoring compliance through the MHRA and GMP guidelines which enables all tests to pass UK market authorisation requirements. Our facilities operate under the Human Medicines Regulations 2012 and the Medicines Act 1968, providing comprehensive compliance with all UK-specific requirements for pharmaceutical products, medical devices, and combination products.

Our organization observes European Medicines Agency (EMA) Clinical Trials Regulation (EU) No 536/2014 international standards together with UK Clinical Trials Framework processes that adjust to post-Brexit regulations. The Pharmaceutical Quality Control procedures at our facility follow the International Council for Harmonisation guidelines from Q1 to Q12 with emphasis on both ICH Q2(R2) for analytical procedure validation and ICH Q6A for new drug substance and product specification testing requirements. We have established ISO/IEC 17025:2017 accreditation for our testing and calibration laboratories in order to prove our dedication to technical competence and results reliability.

Our company extends its standardisation by adopting both European Directorate for the Quality of Medicines (EDQM) standards and British Pharmacopoeia (BP) specifications in addition to these essential frameworks. The Testing Services at our facility follow the UK Medical Devices Regulations 2002 (SI 2002 No 618, as amended) along with the In Vitro Diagnostic Medical Devices Regulations 2000 (SI 2000 No 1315). The Manufacturing Practice and product testing requirements for advanced therapy medicinal products (ATMPs) are managed according to MHRA specifications. The Yellow Card scheme serves us to report adverse events as we maintain full pharmacovigilance and safety monitoring during all product life stages.

Pharmaceutical Products Testing:
1. Medicines and Drugs

We offer Pharmaceutical Testing Services for various forms of medicinal products, ensuring that each dosage form is accurately formulated and meets the necessary pharmacological standards. Our services include testing for:

  • Tablets and Capsules: A series of tests must be performed on the medication to evaluate its uniformity, disintegration, dissolution and stability for achieving the best results.
  • Injectable Drugs (vials, ampoules): Sterility testing, endotoxin testing and stability studies to verify the safety and efficacy of injectables.
  • Liquid Syrups: Testing for viscosity, pH levels, stability and microbiological contamination.
  • Ointments and Creams: Tests will evaluate consistency, spreadability and stability as well as check for microbial contamination for skin compatibility and product integrity.
  • Inhalers and Nebulisers: Performance testing for dose uniformity, aerodynamic properties and product stability.
  • Suppositories and Pessaries: Evaluation for dissolution rate, release profile and stability to ensure effective therapeutic delivery.
2. Packaging Materials

The laboratory performs packaging material testing to protect drug products from integrity risks while ensuring official guidelines remain in compliance. The Pharmaceutical Quality Control Testing services for packaging at our company consist of:

  • Blister Packs for Tablets and Capsules: Assessments for sealing strength, moisture resistance and barrier properties.
  • Plastic Bottles for Syrups and Suspensions: Leak testing, stability assessments and compatibility with the drug product.
  • Ampoules and Vials (Glass and Plastic): Verification of strength, sterilisation compatibility and suitability for specific formulations.
  • IV Bags and Fluid Containers: Evaluation for container integrity, material compatibility, and performance under different conditions.
  • Sealing Films and Wraps: Testing for sealing strength, moisture permeability and protective qualities.
  • Laminated Pouches for Powders and Granules: Barrier testing for moisture, oxygen and light protection.
  • Child-Resistant Caps and Closures: Safety testing to ensure compliance with regulatory requirements for child-resistant features.
